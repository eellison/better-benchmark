## Diagnosis

The oracle computes the complete DINOv2 inference broadcast-scale residual LayerNorm scope in one row-wise Triton kernel with BLOCK_H=1024 (covering the full hidden=768 dimension in one tile), num_warps=8, num_stages=3. It loads addmm, scale, and residual, fuses the broadcast multiply + add, computes population mean/variance with simple sum/divide (not Welford), applies rsqrt + affine, and stores the final [175360, 768] output in one pass.

Inductor emits 1 kernel (`triton_red_fused_add_mul_rsqrt_sub_var_mean_view_0`) that covers the same operations but uses its generic var_mean reduction schedule with different tiling. The Inductor kernel lands at 327.5 us with coordinate descent tuning vs. the oracle's 300 us, a ~9% gap.

The gap arises because Inductor's codegen path does not specialize for the case where the full hidden dimension fits in one tile block (hidden=768 < BLOCK_H=1024), enabling single-pass statistics without Welford state. The oracle eliminates reduction overhead by directly computing mean and variance as `sum(x)/N` and `sum((x-mean)^2)/N` in registers.

The oracle shows that when hidden fits in one tile block, a simple sum-divide approach outperforms the generic reduction schedule.
