# sum_sum_bc10a7742387 Investigation

## Summary
- **Model**: torchbench_timm_vovnet_train_001
- **Ratio**: 1.313x (oracle: 9.09us, compile: 11.94us)
- **Classification**: SCHEDULER_FUSION

## Root Cause

VoVNet BN-backward tail pattern. The oracle consumes the same 8 inputs and computes the ReLU-backward `where(mm / 49)` producer, centered activation, both channel reductions, the returned `[1024]` side vector, and dependent BN-backward full-tensor epilogue in one per-channel Triton kernel.

Inductor cannot form one full-scope multi-output reduction template that shares the masked pointwise producer across sibling sums and sinks the finalized sums into the returned full tensor epilogue.

Shape: [32, 1024, 7, 7] with reduction over [0, 2, 3] = 1568 elements per channel, 1024 channels.

## Kernel Count
- **Oracle**: 1-2 kernels (fused dual reduction + epilogue)
- **Inductor**: 1 kernel but with sub-optimal tiling for multi-output

## Config Exploration
coordinate_descent_tuning=True gives 10.4us, combo_kernels regresses to 12.9us. The gap is modest (1.31x) for a small absolute time (9us oracle).

## Key Files
- `/tmp/pytorch-work/torch/_inductor/scheduler.py` - multi-output reduction template
- `/tmp/pytorch-work/torch/_inductor/codegen/triton.py` - dual accumulator codegen

## Design Doc

With 1024 channels and only 1568 elements per channel reduction, this is a relatively small problem. The oracle's advantage is in sharing element loads across two accumulators and computing the epilogue in-place with the reduction results. The fix is the same multi-output reduction template that would benefit sum_sum_67d7103962e7 and sum_sum_b16afac198fb, but the impact here is modest due to the small reduction dimension.
