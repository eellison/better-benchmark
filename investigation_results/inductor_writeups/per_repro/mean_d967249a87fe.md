## Root Cause

The oracle computes BN-inference affine, SiLU activation, and 8x8 spatial mean in one fused Triton reduction kernel that writes the final contiguous [128, 640] output directly. The kernel tiles over (batch, channel) and reduces the HW=64 spatial dimension in-register, applying per-channel BN parameters (reused across the spatial tile).

Inductor also produces 1 kernel (`triton_per_fused_add_div_exp_mean_mul_neg_reciprocal_sqrt_sub_unsqueeze_0`) that fuses all operations together. The kernel operates on xnumel=81920 (128*640) with r0_numel=64 (spatial).

The gap is small (1.185x) and comes from codegen quality differences:
1. The oracle precomputes the BN affine (scale/shift) from the 1D parameters once per channel, then applies them within the spatial reduction loop. Inductor's generic codegen loads and recomputes the per-channel parameters within the reduction body.
2. The oracle uses shape-specialized constants (exactly 64 spatial elements, HW=8*8) which allows the compiler to optimize the reduction loop and eliminate masking overhead.
3. The oracle uses `tl.dot` semantics implicitly through the tiled access pattern.

## Config Exploration
- `coordinate_descent_tuning=True`: Already enabled

## Why Inductor Cannot Fully Close This Gap Today

Inductor already fuses correctly but its generic reduction template:
1. Does not hoist broadcast-invariant loads (per-channel BN params) out of the reduction inner loop
2. Does not specialize the spatial size to eliminate masking when HW is a power of 2

## Required Enhancement (Design Doc)

**Broadcast-Invariant Hoisting in Reduction Codegen**: The Triton codegen in `torch/_inductor/codegen/triton.py` should detect when inputs are broadcast along the reduction dimension (e.g., [C] parameters broadcast to [N,C,H,W] then reduced over H,W) and hoist their loads outside the reduction loop.

This is a relatively small codegen improvement that would benefit all BN-affine + spatial reduction patterns.
