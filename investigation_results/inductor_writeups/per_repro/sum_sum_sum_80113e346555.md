## Exact fusion rejections (file:line)

1. K2+K3 seam (op3 vs op4, op9 vs op10, and fused op1_op7_op3_op9 vs op4_op10):
   `cannot fuse: numel/rnumel mismatch (non-reduce) (2048, 131072), (1, 1)`
   -> `torch/_inductor/codegen/simd.py:2061` (SIMDScheduling.can_fuse, pointwise-pointwise
   branch requires numel1 == numel2 exactly).
2. K0+K3 (op0 vs op4 / op0_op2_op8 vs op4_op10):
   `cannot fuse: intermediate nodes between node1 & node2`
   -> `torch/_inductor/scheduler.py:8787` (Scheduler.can_fuse_vertical; op1/op3 are
   unscheduled intermediates between the reduction and the epilogue).
3. op0 vs op1 (reduction -> channel-group pointwise): `no shared data`
   (score_fusion_memory = 0 because op1 reads buf0[(x//1)*4+k]-style unrolled deps that
   do not match op0's write dep buf0[x]); and would be truly illegal at x=8192 tiling
   anyway since the group sum reads buf0 across 4 different x elements.

## Why full single-kernel (oracle) plan is out of reach for generic codegen
Oracle tiles EVERYTHING at x=[64,32]=2048, r=64, keeping the [64,128] channel sums as
4 per-sub-axis register partials. Inductor cannot express a graph buffer that is a
partial r-sub-axis reduction inside another reduction's tile (MultiOutputReduction /
nested reduction redesign). The K0 x=8192 tiling makes group sums cross-thread reads,
so the scheduler's rejection of op0+op4 is legality-correct, not just heuristic.

## Fixable seam
K2 (x=2048) -> K3 (x=131072 = 2048*64): the epilogue reads the coefficient buffers
with broadcast indexing buf[x//64]. Fusing means the coefficient computation is
recomputed (or redundantly stored) per 64-element tile — exactly what the existing
`expand_dimension_for_pointwise_nodes` machinery (scheduler.py:8215,
config.expand_dimension_for_pointwise_nodes=False) supports via Mod indexing on the
smaller node. Plan: relax/enable this path behind a new config flag for the
producer-consumer-broadcast case (consumer numel = k * producer numel), measure.
Expected: 4 -> 3 kernels (K2 disappears into K3).
