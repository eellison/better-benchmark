## Diagnosis

The oracle computes the complete NFNet channel-wise sum with a two-stage Triton reduction that hoists the two [N,C,1,1] broadcast operands (sigmoid and getitem) outside each H/W tile, performing the per-element f32 multiply, scale by 1/2304, add, and spatial reduction in a single pass with broadcast-scalar caching. Inductor lowers the same expression as a generic staged reduction with three kernels and vector-shaped reloads of broadcast operands, not recognizing the small per-channel broadcast pattern.

## Root cause

Inductor's reduction scheduler does not form a tile schedule with broadcast-scalar hoisting for this NCHW layout. The computation is `sum((x * sigmoid + getitem/2304), dim=[0,2,3])` with sigmoid and getitem being [N,C,1,1] broadcast tensors. The oracle loads each channel's broadcast pair once per tile (evict_last policy), then processes 1024 spatial elements per tile with those scalars held in registers, writing partials. Inductor generates a generic multi-kernel staged reduction that reloads the broadcast operands per element or per row.
